Mentors

Bryce Jensen Dietrich

Associate Professor, Department of Political Science Research Scholar, Center for C-SPAN Scholarship and Engagement (CCSE)

Dr. Dietrich uses novel quantitative, automated, and machine learning methods to analyze non-traditional data sources such as audio (or speech) data and video data. These methods are used to understand the causes and consequences of elite and mass political behavior. His work has received considerable support from the National Institute of Health (NIH) and National Science Foundation (NSF).It has also been covered by NPR, Washington Post, FiveThirtyEight, The Economist and The Wall Street Journal.

David Goldberg

Purdue University

David Goldberg is a Professor of Mathematics at Purdue University and has a well-established research career in pure math. Through his work with the Math Alliance, first as a Mentor (2009-), then as its Executive Director (2016-) he has worked to broaden participation in STEM. As part of this work, he has become very interested in emerging sciences and how it could transform the profession. He spends a great deal of time advocating for mathematicians to become more involved in these data driven fields, and to explore research with undergraduates. In this project he functioned mostly as an observer, trying to learn more about what tools are used to help us understand what data can tell us. He is very impressed with what the students, Dr. Dietrich, and Mr. Chari have been able to accomplish this year, and wants to thank them all for tolerating him and allowing him to lurk about.
